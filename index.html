  • POP [ULIST] WORDS


    Is there a common language in populist rhetoric? Browse to find some answers.

  • The Pop Words Project

    Right wing populist leaders have sprung across the world, unprecedently amassing millions of fans and followers. These leaders are thought to share very similar ways of communicating with their intended audiences. But exactly what political strategies and rhetorics underlie their popularity?

    Pop Words tackles these questions by analyzing a corpus of speeches by populist right-wing leaders like Trump, Salvini and Bolsonaro. We work in the domain of political rhetoric analysis through close and distant reading methods such as rhetorical, topic modelling, sentiment, and lexical analysis. We then create a formal ontology to conceptualize and formalize rhetorical strategies employed by Right wing populists (RWPs). While we first analyze and formalize the speech acts of RWPs, this ontology can be exploited for a variety of political content and speech acts, regardless of political affiliation.

  • The Initial Corpus

    In order to develop an ontology of political rhetorics, we decided to start by focusing on speech acts by three of the most popular RWP leaders of the moment: President Donald Trump of the US, (Ex) Minister of the Interior Matteo Salvini of Italy, and President Jair Bolsonaro of Brazil.

    Specifically, we decided to build the ontology based on data gathered from close and distant reading of a small corpus made of speeches by these three leaders. The speeches selected include:

    • Trump’s Inaugural Address (January 20th, 2017, Washington DC, USA)
      •
    • Trump’s Minnesota Rally Speech (October 10th, 2019, Minneapolis, USA)
      •
    • Salvini’s Speech to the Senate (August 20th, 2019, Rome, Italy)
      •
    • Salvini’s Piazza Duomo Speech (February 24th, 2018, Milan, Italy)
      •
    • Bolsonaro’s Inaugural Address (January 2st, 2019, Brasilia, Brazil)
      •
    • Bolsonaro’s Opening of the 74th UN General Assembly (September 24th, 2019, New York, USA)
      •
    • Bolsonaro’s Address for the ceremony of new Minister of Defense General Fernando Azevedo (January 2nd, 2019, Brasilia, Brazil)
      •

    We decided to include a third speech by Bolsonaro in order to achieve a more balanced corpus, as Bolsonaro is known for giving short and succinct speeches.

Distant Reading

In order to gain a better understanding of the explicit and implicit information present in the corpus, we analyzed the corpus through distant reading. We were interested in taking note of trends only perceivable at larger levels of analysis—such as the most common topics, words, and sentences, the kinds of topics the speeches contain and share, the emotional content, as well as the level of complexity of the texts.
